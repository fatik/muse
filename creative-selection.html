Creative Selection - Book review

Creative Selection by Ken Kocienda offers a fascinating insider's view of Apple's product development process during some of its most innovative years. As a former Apple engineer who worked on groundbreaking projects like Safari and the iPhone keyboard, Kocienda provides unique insights into the company's creative process.

Key Themes

1. Demo-Driven Development

Kocienda emphasizes Apple's reliance on working demos rather than lengthy presentations or documentation. This approach, which he calls "creative selection," involves rapidly iterating through prototypes and getting frequent feedback from decision-makers.

2. Seven Essential Elements

  • Inspiration: Drawing ideas from various sources
  • Collaboration: Working closely with talented teammates
  • Craft: Paying attention to every detail
  • Diligence: Persistent refinement of ideas
  • Decisiveness: Making clear choices and moving forward
  • Taste: Developing an intuition for what feels right
  • Empathy: Understanding user needs and experiences

3. The Importance of Taste

Throughout the book, Kocienda emphasizes how Apple's success relied heavily on developing good taste - the ability to distinguish between good and great solutions. This wasn't just about aesthetic preferences but about making technology both powerful and accessible.

Notable Projects

The iPhone Keyboard

One of the most fascinating sections details the development of the iPhone's virtual keyboard. Kocienda describes the numerous challenges and iterations required to create a typing experience that would work on a touchscreen - something that had never been done successfully before.

Safari Web Browser

The author also shares insights into the development of Safari, highlighting how Apple approached building a web browser from scratch and the technical challenges involved.

Critical Analysis

Strengths:

  • Provides rare insights into Apple's development process
  • Offers practical lessons applicable to any creative work
  • Balances technical details with accessible storytelling

Limitations:

  • Focuses primarily on software development
  • Limited to the author's personal experience
  • Some readers might find the technical details overwhelming

Final Verdict

Creative Selection is an invaluable read for anyone interested in product development, software engineering, or Apple's history. While it's particularly relevant for technology professionals, its lessons about creativity, iteration, and decision-making are broadly applicable.

The book successfully demystifies Apple's development process while providing practical insights that readers can apply to their own work. It's both a historical document of a crucial period in technology and a practical guide to creative problem-solving.

Rating: 4.5/5 - A must-read for anyone interested in technology development and creative processes, particularly in the context of building user-focused products.
